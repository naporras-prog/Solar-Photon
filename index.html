Silicon wafers in solar cells absorb photons whose energy exceeds the silicon bandgap (1.1 eV). This excites electrons from the valence band to the conduction band.

These free electrons create a voltage across the p-n junction.

Solar panels output direct current (DC). Inverters use electronic switching to convert DC into alternating current (AC) matching grid frequency (60 Hz).

Modern microinverters optimize output per panel, improving efficiency in shaded or uneven conditions.

Excess power flows to the grid, while shortages draw from either the grid or battery storage.
